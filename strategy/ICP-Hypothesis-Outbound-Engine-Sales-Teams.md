## The Hypothesis

Companies with 10-80 employees that have sales or BD people in place but no sophisticated outbound system represent a high-value market for the Complete Outbound Engine. They are already spending money on outbound (salaries, tools, time) but getting poor ROI because their salespeople are doing manual prospecting work that a system should handle.

**The gap:** Big enough to have dedicated sales people. Too small for enterprise sales ops, RevOps teams, or Salesforce admins. Running outbound on gut feel, manual LinkedIn, and basic email tools.

**Validated by:** Direct intel from a BD Consultant at Aeterna Power Limited (specialist data centre manpower provider, 10 employees, incorporated August 2024). Confirmed that research is entirely manual, outreach is manual, they want to automate everything, and they want to be seen as AI-forward but lack the technical capability.

## The Two Personas

This segment has a split buying motion. The person who feels the pain is not the person who signs the cheque.

### Sam (the Buyer, MD/CEO)

The decision-maker at a B2B company with a small sales team doing outbound manually.

- Age 35-55
- Revenue £500k-5M+, deal sizes £10k-3.5M+
- 10-80 employees, dedicated sales/BD team of 2-10 reps
- Each rep doing £200k-500k+ GP per year
- Cares about output per rep, competitive positioning, scaling without adding headcount
- Wants to be seen as tech-forward and AI-forward
- Buying triggers: sales team hitting capacity ceiling, competitors adopting AI, reps spending 60%+ of time on research and admin, quiet months, reading about AI automation and asking "why aren't we doing this?"
- What they want: outbound infrastructure their sales team runs on. Built, tested, handed over. Optional retainer.

### John BD (the Champion, BD Rep/SDR)

The person who feels the daily pain. Not the buyer, but the internal advocate who sells the project upward to Sam.

- Age 25-40
- Title: Business Development Consultant, BD Manager, SDR, Account Executive
- Wears multiple hats at a small company
- High-performing individually (£500k+ GP possible) but limited by manual processes
- Daily pain: finding the right people within target businesses is all manual
- Wants to automate: manual outreach, data formatting, research, sourcing, list building
- Dreams of scraping their entire market and adding everyone to a CRM
- Uses ChatGPT for market research but nothing connected to actual workflows
- Cold calling is primary channel, email and LinkedIn supplementary but underinvested
- Does not control budget but has strong influence on what gets bought

## Two Sub-Types

### 1. Growth-Stage (SaaS/tech startups, new specialist firms)

Getting going, no system in place. Need everything from scratch.

- B2B SaaS startups (Series A/B, 20-80 people)
- AI/tech startups with a sales hire but no infrastructure
- New specialist services firms (like Aeterna Power, incorporated recently, growing fast)
- Have hired 3-8 SDRs but the "system" is a CRM seat and a bought list
- Founder originally did sales themselves, now struggling to systematise what they did instinctively
- Data quality is terrible. SDRs waste hours finding contacts manually
- Big pain: high SDR cost, low conversion, no personalisation at scale

### 2. Old-School (manufacturers, established services, traditional industries)

Have a sales process that works but need to reach far more people. Have legacy systems, want modernisation and scale.

- Manufacturing/product companies with field sales or inside sales teams
- Industrial/trade supply companies (20-100 staff)
- Still relying on trade shows, directories, word of mouth, and cold calling
- Have field sales reps and inside sales but almost no digital outbound
- Massive untapped market because nobody is selling them modern outbound tooling
- Less sophisticated buyers but very loyal once results show

## Horizontal Industry Segments

### B2B Recruitment and Staffing Agencies (10-50 staff)

The segment validated by Aeterna Power. Almost always have SDRs/BDRs doing manual outreach. High volume, low personalisation. Templated InMails all day. Revenue depends directly on pipeline volume. Rarely have Clay/enrichment tooling. Most use LinkedIn Recruiter and nothing else.

**Specific to specialist/technical recruitment:**
- Market is exploding (data centres alone: £36bn UK pipeline, 439,000 workers needed globally by 2027)
- Cross-border compliance adds complexity that manual processes cannot handle at scale
- Competitors include V7 Recruitment, Navartis, DataX Connect, First Point Group
- The BD process is relationship and project-tracking driven, but cold email to project directors and hiring managers is a natural fit
- At 10 people, they are doing BD manually: LinkedIn, events, personal network. Huge room for a systemised approach

### B2B SaaS in Growth Stage (Series A/B, 20-80 people)

Have hired 3-8 SDRs but the "system" is a Salesloft/Outreach seat and a bought list. Founder originally did sales themselves, now struggling to systematise what they did instinctively. Data quality is terrible. SDRs waste hours finding contacts manually. Big pain: high SDR cost, low conversion, no personalisation at scale.

### AI/Tech Startups (10-50 staff)

Positioning themselves as tech-forward but using manual processes internally. The irony is the angle. They understand the value of automation because that is what they sell, but have not applied it to their own sales process. Often have technical co-founders who appreciate the Clay/automation approach.

### IT Services / Managed Service Providers (15-60 staff)

Have BD people but outbound is mostly networking, events, and cold calling. Email outreach is basic. Bought lists, generic templates. Technical enough to appreciate automation but have not built it. Sticky once you are in because the system becomes part of their sales infrastructure.

### Digital/Creative Agencies (15-40 staff)

Often have a new business person or small BD team. Outbound is usually one person doing manual LinkedIn plus referrals. Feast-or-famine revenue cycles. Exactly the problem the system solves. They understand personalisation value because they sell creative work.

### Commercial Insurance Brokers / Financial Services (Mid-Market)

Relationship-heavy sales but increasingly need volume. BDMs doing manual prospecting with nothing more than a CRM and phone. Regulated enough that they cannot just spam. Personalisation and compliance matter. High deal values make the ROI obvious.

### Industrial/Trade Supply Companies (20-100 staff)

Have field sales reps and inside sales but almost no digital outbound. Still relying on trade shows, directories, and word of mouth. Massive untapped market because nobody is selling them modern outbound tooling. Less sophisticated buyers but very loyal once results show.

### Professional Services Firms (Accountancy, Legal, Consulting, 10-50 staff)

Partners expect "business development" but the BD team has no system. Usually doing events, content, and hoping for referrals. High deal values, long sales cycles. Signal-based targeting is incredibly valuable here.

## Vertical Segment: Hardware / Electronics

Consumer electronics, MedTech, and IoT companies with a B2B sales motion. They built something brilliant, raised money to sell it, then realised selling is a completely different skill.

- 15-80 employees, $3M+ raised or $2M+ revenue
- Post-V1 product needing sales volume, no sales ops or RevOps leader
- Industries: consumer electronics, MedTech, IoT (only B2B sales motion, not pure D2C)
- Fundable and funded (budget exists), time-pressured (runway, product lifecycle, competitive window)
- Technical enough to appreciate automation, selling complex products where personalisation matters
- Often completely ignored by outbound agencies (who focus on SaaS)

**Buying triggers specific to hardware/electronics:**
- Just closed Series A/B (new sales mandate from investors)
- Hired first VP Sales or Head of BD in last 6 months
- Exhibiting at CES, MedTech conferences (need meetings booked before the event)
- V1 launched 6-12 months ago, now need distribution/enterprise deals
- Competitor just raised a bigger round (urgency)

## Universal Qualifying Signals (What to Look for in Clay)

- 10-80 employees
- Job titles like "SDR", "BDR", "Business Development", "Inside Sales" in their team or recent hires
- Using HubSpot or Pipedrive (not Salesforce + Outreach + 6Sense, that is enterprise)
- Recently hired sales leadership (new Head of Sales = mandate to improve pipeline)
- No "RevOps", "Sales Ops", "Sales Engineer" hires (those signals mean they are already sophisticated)
- Company positioning mentions AI, automation, or tech-forward but their sales stack does not reflect it
- Cold calling is mentioned as primary channel (email and LinkedIn underinvested)

**The killer qualifier:** "Do your SDRs build their own lists?"
If yes, they need us. That means there is no system, no enrichment, no automation. Their salespeople are doing research work instead of selling.

---

## Disqualifying Signals (Too Sophisticated Already)

- Hiring for RevOps, Sales Operations, or Sales Enablement roles
- Using Salesforce + Outreach/Salesloft + intent data tools (6Sense, Bombora)
- Have a dedicated data/enrichment team
- 100+ employees with a VP Sales who has been in role 2+ years
- Already running Clay or similar enrichment tooling
- Have an in-house marketing team with demand gen capabilities

---

## Buying Triggers (Across All Segments)

- New Head of Sales or VP Sales hired in last 6 months
- Recently raised funding (Series A/B for SaaS/hardware, growth capital for services)
- Job posts for SDRs or BDRs (scaling outbound manually)
- Founder posting about pipeline challenges on LinkedIn
- Competitor in their space just raised or expanded
- Trade show or conference coming up (need meetings booked)
- End of quarter pipeline pressure
- Recently lost a key client or revenue dip (urgency)
- Company publicly positioning as AI-forward but sales process is manual (the irony angle)

## The Outreach Approach: Two Angles

This segment requires a dual-angle campaign approach because the person who feels the pain is not the person who signs the cheque.

### Angle 1: Bottom-Up (Target John BD)

Target BD reps, SDRs, and Account Executives with pain-focused copy. They champion the project internally.

**Copy themes:**
- "Still researching prospects by hand?"
- "Your reps spend more time on LinkedIn searches than actual conversations"
- "What would your week look like if every morning started with a fresh list of qualified prospects?"

**Goal:** Get John BD to think "this is exactly what I need" and take it to their MD.

### Angle 2: Top-Down (Target Sam)

Target MDs, CEOs, and Founders with outcome-focused copy. They approve the spend.

**Copy themes:**
- "Your sales team could cover 3x more accounts without hiring"
- "Every rep you add costs £40k+. What if the ones you have could just reach more people?"
- "You want to be AI-forward. Here is what that actually looks like for your sales team."

**Goal:** Get Sam to see the ROI and competitive positioning argument. Frame as infrastructure investment, not a service.

The hypothesis was validated by direct intel from a BD Consultant at Aeterna Power Limited, a specialist data centre manpower/workforce deployment provider.

**Company profile:**

**Market context:**

- Demand for cooling/HVAC engineers up 67% since 2022
- Competitors: V7 Recruitment (1,000+ contractors mobilised), Navartis (10,000+ placed since 2006), DataX Connect, First Point Group

**What the BD Consultant confirmed:**

4. The buying motion is split: champion (rep) + buyer (MD).
5. This pattern applies across specialist recruitment, technical consulting, engineering, SaaS, tech startups, manufacturing, and any B2B services firm with a small dedicated sales team.
