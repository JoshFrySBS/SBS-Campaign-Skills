# BD Friend Interview — Template

**Goal:** validate the Sam-ICP hypothesis with someone living it daily. Capture the *why* behind hiring, manual processes, and the unbuilt system. The output feeds the DFY Engine's research prompts and copy library.

**Format:** 30-minute call (or async voice notes). Keep it conversational, not a survey.
**Save to:** `bd-friend-interview-{YYYY-MM-DD}.md` in this folder.

---

## Pre-call setup

Quick LinkedIn scan of the friend's company (3 mins):
- Company size + industry
- Number of BDRs / SDRs visible on LinkedIn
- Founder background (delivery / sales / tech?)
- Recent press / funding / hires
- Their job title + how long in role

Note anything that already validates or contradicts the Sam hypothesis before the call. Don't lead with these — let them tell you.

---

## Interview structure

Five sections, ~6 minutes each. Push for stories and specifics, not opinions.

### 1. The hire — why does the role exist?

- "Why did [the company] hire a BD function rather than another delivery person, marketer, or partnerships hire?"
- "Who was the original sales person before you? Founder? An old AE? A friend?"
- "What was the trigger — was it a quiet quarter, a churned client, a board ask?"
- "When you joined, was there an existing playbook to follow or did you build it?"

**Listen for:** founder pain, growth ceiling, visible "we tried referrals and they ran out" moments. These become Email 1 hooks.

### 2. A real day — what does the work actually look like?

- "Walk me through last Wednesday. What did you spend most of the day on?"
- "What % of your week is research / list building / formatting / sending vs actual conversations?"
- "Where does a 'good' lead come from — is it a list someone gave you, a Sales Nav search, a referral, a LinkedIn ping?"
- "How many people are you reaching out to in a typical week? How many actually hear back?"

**Listen for:** the words "manually", "by hand", "in a spreadsheet", "I copy/paste", "I use ChatGPT to..." — these confirm the manual thesis directly.

### 3. The tools — what's there and what's missing?

- "What does your current stack look like? CRM, anything for finding people, anything for sending?"
- "Why did you pick those tools? Or were they already there when you joined?"
- "What's a tool you've heard about that you'd love to have but don't?"
- "Have you ever tried Apollo, Clay, Outreach, Salesloft, Instantly, Cognism? What was your impression?"

**Listen for:** "we use Sales Nav and that's it" / "we have HubSpot but no one uses it for outbound" / "I use ChatGPT for research but not connected to anything." These are the manual-thesis confirmation lines.

### 4. The unbuilt system — why hasn't it happened yet?

- "Has anyone in the business ever tried to set up a proper outbound system? What happened?"
- "If you went to your MD tomorrow and said 'we need to build this', what's the response?"
- "Has the company ever bought outbound services from an agency? What was the outcome?"
- "Why do you think — honestly — this hasn't been built already? Time? Budget? Skills? Belief?"

**Listen for:** the real reason. Often it's "no one has the technical chops" or "we don't have a sales ops hire and we never will at this size" or "the founder doesn't trust agencies after [x]." This is gold for the copy library.

### 5. The buying frame — what would actually move them?

- "Imagine someone emailed your MD with a system that did the manual stuff for the team. What would the MD's first reaction be?"
- "What would make them say 'let me see this' vs delete?"
- "Who else in the business would they need to talk to before saying yes?"
- "If a £10–15k system showed up that built outbound for the team, would that be a no-brainer, a stretch, or a non-starter? Why?"
- "What would they think if they saw 'most teams hit the manual ceiling within 6 months' — does that resonate?"

**Listen for:** the buying objection AND the buying belief. Both go into Email 1 / Email 2 angles.

---

## Wrap-up questions (last 2 minutes)

- "If I sent an outbound email to your MD, what would you say *not* to put in it?"
- "Anyone else in your network you think we should talk to who's living the same problem?"
- "Anything I didn't ask that I should have?"
